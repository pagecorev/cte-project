Symptoms of CTE

Understanding how CTE affects behavior, emotions, and physical health

Emotional and Internal Brain Related Symptoms

CTE can lead to memory loss, confusion, and difficulty thinking clearly. Emotional changes include depression, irritability, and variability in mood. Although these are common they usually develop after a while of having the disease.

These changes often appear years after repeated head impacts (small and large), making early diagnosis challenging.

Emotional and cognitive changes are common in individuals with CTE.

Physical Symptoms

Headaches, dizziness, and problems with motor skills may appear over time. These symptoms can interfere with daily life and athletic performance.

Tracking these signs in athletes can help with prevention and early intervention.

Physical symptoms often manifest gradually but can significantly impact life quality.

Diagnosing CTE

It is important to note that CTE is only able to be diagnosed once the person is already dead, and when the doctors can directly look at the brain (X-rays and other imaging technology does not currently work at the moment and can only provide doctors with limited information)

The way doctors, specifically Neuropathologists, identify CTE is through looking at major altercations in the brain, which most commonly means CTE, but sometimes it could also mean other things such as Alzheimer's Disease.
