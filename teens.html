Day in the Life

A look at how CTE impacts daily life for teens and athletes

Daily Challenges

CTE can affect school, social life, and sports participation. Teens may struggle with concentration, memory, and emotional regulation.

Many people suggest getting therapy, especially in younger people as they are more susceptible to the drastic emotional changes.

Family support plays a critical role in managing daily challenges.

How Friends and Family Can Help

Understanding CTE, and how to improve one's symptoms is key to helping all people struggling with the disease.

Suggesting getting help from professionals can give people the support they need to seek help, while helping with daily things such as homework or routine tasks like chores, can help reduce stress allowing some to recover more quickly.

Sports & Activities

Even with CTE, many teens continue participating in sports safely. While this practice is generally okay, it is important for teens to be aware of the concussion protocols and take proper care of their bodies when they participate.

Staying healthy during any type of recovery process is also essential. Making sure that people don't do drugs or are involved in risky environments such as parties can also help some recover better, and prevent future cases of CTE.

Modified training and awareness can reduce risk during practice.
